About me

A desire to do good

Having spent a large part of my career in roles revolving policy and community research, I am deeply vested and rooted in community issues. My passion for doing good has led me to volunteer as a Python teacher at Code in the Community, and also as a volunteer developer in the Techladies Bootcamp, where I was part of a team that helped to build a Volunteer Management System for the non-profit organisation, It's Raining Raincoats. It was from these volunteer experiences that I grew to love tech, and I'm looking for an opportunity to merge both my passion for the community, with my love for tech.

Endlessly curious — a DIY mindset

Not one to be comfortable with status quo, I am always asking myself if there are ways to do things better, or more efficiently. For example, while powerpoint slides and charts are a quintessential tool in a consultant's toolkit, the charting of figures and tables itself is a mindless and draining task. Asking the question of "can I do this better?" drove me to write a Python script that automates the creation of stacked bar charts and tables within Powerpoint, reducing the time taken to chart by almost 80%. Check out my script on Github

A happy wearer of many hats

Having worked in a start-up before, I relish in the ever-evolving nature of job roles, and I'm not afraid to dabble in areas that are beyond my current set of expertise. An area that I am particularly interested in helping out with is with the more technical aspects of the platform, including software engineering. I am currently building my capacity in development, and would love to help out and learn more! While I am not trained in computer science, I did play the informal role of technical support in a previous role, and am participating in a bootcamp where I am part of a team that is building a Volunteer Management System using Vue.js + Node.js.
